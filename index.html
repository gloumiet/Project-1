Coffee

The Heart of Your Cup

Coffee is the base of the drink and sets the overall flavor. The taste depends on the type of bean, roast level, and brewing method. Light roasts are brighter and more acidic, medium roasts are balanced, and dark roasts are bold and rich. Freshly brewed coffee or espresso gives the best flavor and aroma.

Milk

Creamy Goodness

Milk adds creaminess and softens the bitterness of coffee. Whole milk makes drinks rich and smooth, while low-fat milk is lighter. Plant-based options like oat, almond, or soy milk each add their own flavor, oat milk is especially popular for its creamy texture and ability to foam well.

Toppings

Flavor Your Way

Toppings and add-ins customize the drink and add sweetness or extra flavor. Common options include flavored syrups like vanilla or caramel, sugar or honey, spices such as cinnamon or nutmeg, chocolate drizzle, or whipped cream. These extras turn a basic coffee into a personalized drink.
